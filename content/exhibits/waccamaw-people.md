The Waccamaw Indian People are **South Carolina's first state-recognized tribe**, recognized by the State of South Carolina in **2005**. In the tribe's own words:

> The Waccamaw Indian People are South Carolina's first state-recognized tribe (2005), with over 300 years of continuous presence in the Dog Bluff community near Aynor. We preserve our cultural heritage, maintain tribal sovereignty, and support our community through educational programs, cultural events, and traditional practices.

## More than 300 years at Dog Bluff

For over 300 years, the Waccamaw people have called the **Dog Bluff community** near **Aynor, South Carolina**, home. The tribal grounds are located at 591 Bluewater Rd, Aynor, SC. The tribe describes itself as a people with "a proud civil rights tradition we intend to carry forward."

## A living culture

The Waccamaw Indian People work to preserve their cultural heritage and maintain tribal sovereignty, supporting the community through educational programs, cultural events, and traditional practices. As the tribe puts it, their annual gathering "celebrates our living culture and welcomes visitors to experience authentic indigenous traditions."

Community efforts described on the tribe's site include cultural programs and classes, tribal meetings, youth education and Youth Days, school days, and community support services such as food bank and mobile pantry distributions.

## The Annual Pauwau & Cultural Arts Festival

Each year the tribe hosts the **Waccamaw Pauwau & Cultural Arts Festival** on the Waccamaw Tribal Grounds in Aynor. The **34th Annual Pauwau** is scheduled for **November 7–8, 2026**. The Pauwau is an open celebration of the tribe's living culture and a welcome to visitors.

## "We Are Still Here"

The tribe continues to pursue **federal recognition** and to share its story publicly. A phrase the community uses to sum up its spirit: *#WeAreStillHere.*
